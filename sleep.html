Sleep and Caloric Intake

Sleep quality affects appetite hormones. Inadequate sleep can disrupt the balance between them, potentially leading to increased cravings for high-calorie foods and impacting the absorbtion of energy

When sleep-deprived, individuals often experience alterations in these hormones, leading to increased appetite and cravings for high-calorie, sugary foods.

Consequently, insufficient sleep can disrupt the delicate balance of caloric intake, potentially resulting in overeating and weight gain.
