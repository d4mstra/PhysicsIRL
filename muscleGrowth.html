Calories and Muscle Growth

Caloric intake supports muscle growth by providing the necessary energy and macronutrients for the synthesis of new muscle tissue. In a state of caloric surplus, the body has enough resources to repair damaged muscle fibers which contributes to overall muscle hypertrophy.

In addition to providing energy for muscle repair and growth, caloric intake also plays a crucial role in fueling the various physiological processes involved in muscle development.

Carbohydrates, fats, and proteins obtained from food serve as the building blocks for muscle tissue synthesis and repair.
